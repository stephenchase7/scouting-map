I. Overview

Player Development, L.L.C. ("PDev"), a subsidiary of Major League Soccer, L.L.C. ("MLS"), organizes, manages, and administers an elite youth development platform ("Program" or "League" operating as "MLS NEXT Homegrown Division") with the mission of providing world class training, development, education, and competition opportunities for select soccer players between the age groups of U13 to U19 across the United States and Canada. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division also provides a professional player development and an elite development pathway for future college, professional, and international players. The Program includes a league, postseason competition, as well as other development-focused initiatives.

II. Definitions

Capitalized terms shall have the definitions ascribed to them in this section. Capitalized terms not defined in this section but defined in other sections of these Rules and Regulations or in other Rules and Policies (as defined below) will have the meaning ascribed to them in those sections or documents.

a. Club (or "Member")

Any soccer club whose application for membership in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division has been accepted, and that has signed a Membership Agreement and paid a performance bond in the amount directed by PDev.

b. Club Representative

Any Club personnel, including but not limited to, any Club employees, Coaches, trainers, Team administrators, other support or volunteer staff, or any individual who represents themselves as being affiliated with a Club in a manner that is known or should be known to the Club, that assist the Club in connection with the Season.

c. Coach

An individual who performs the duties and services as may be assigned to an individual by a Club regarding the training of youth soccer players, including, but not limited to:

  • (i) conducting and supervising the Team's practices, training camps, and training sessions
  • (ii) coaching the Team in all competitions and Matches
  • (iii) preparing for all Matches played by the Team, including developing Match strategies
  • (iv) overseeing off-season Player development programs for the Team
  • (v) consulting and advising with respect to the Team's Roster and Player trades
  • (vi) directing and supervising the Team's coaching staff
  • (vii) enforcing Club and League rules
  • (viii) participating in promotional, sponsorship, and public relations activities

d. Competitive Season

The period of time between the first official Match scheduled by PDev and the final official Match scheduled by PDev (including MLS NEXT Cup).

e. Conference

The grouping of Teams that compete against each other in the Regular Season and whose performance and standings are tracked and reported together.

f. CSA

The Canadian Soccer Association, the governing body for soccer in Canada.

g. Facility

A venue for Team training and Matches, including all parking, structures, and grounds on or immediately surrounding the venue.

h. FIFA

The Fédération Internationale de Football Association (International Federation of Association Football), the international governing body of association soccer.

i. Fixture

A set of scheduled Matches of multiple age groups versus one opponent.

j. Forfeit

A 3-0 loss for the team that is issued the forfeiture for a Match.

k. Future Players

A Player who is Rostered with a Club who is not ready to contribute at the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division level full time.

l. Infraction

Any violation or alleged violation of the Rules and Policies, or any action or alleged action that is against the spirit of the Rules and Policies.

m. Lifetime Ban

Permanently prohibiting an individual from being a Participant.

n. Match/Game

Any soccer match played between two Clubs, or any match organized by PDev, as part of an MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition.

o. Match Day Roster

The official match day roster that each Team must create and submit to the referee prior to a Match, which for U13 through U19 lists 18 Players from the Roster who will be eligible to participate in that Match as well as all individuals who will be in the Club's technical area at any point during the Match.

p. Match Decision

Events or matters that have a direct effect on Matches, excluding disciplinary matters, including, but not limited to, the eligibility of Players, the state of the field, accessory Match equipment, crowd behavior, and stadium installations.

q. Match Official

The referee, assistant referees, fourth official, match commissioner, referee inspector, the person in charge of safety, and/or any other persons appointed by PDev and/or Professional Referee Organization ("PRO") to assume responsibility in connection with a Match.

r. Match Report

A form submitted by a Match Official that records Match data on the field of play, including, but not limited to, a win, loss, tie, goals scored for, goals scored against, total points achieved, misconduct, suspensions, and injuries.

s. Match Suspension

A ban that prevents a Player, Club Representative (in the technical area), or other Participant from taking part in any future Match or MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition, including, but not limited to, being present in the area immediately surrounding the field of play. The suspension is imposed in terms of Matches, days, or months.

t. Membership Agreement

The document that details the rights and obligations of each Club; a fully executed Membership Agreement is required for a Club to become a valid member of MLS NEXT Homegrown Division.

u. MLS Academy

An institution that focuses on youth player development and is owned and operated by a Club that competes in Major League Soccer.

v. MLS NEXT Cup

The MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition held at the conclusion of the Regular Season among the qualifying Teams from the U15 through U19 age groups, culminating in a champion in the U15, U16, U17, and U19 age groups.

w. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition

The MLS NEXT Homegrown Division schedule, collectively referring to friendly matches, Regular Season matches, MLS NEXT Flex, Generation adidas Cup, MLS NEXT Fest, MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Showcases, All-Star Games, and MLS NEXT Cup (each as applicable), featuring at least one Club and organized by PDev.

x. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events

This definition includes, but is not limited to, MLS NEXT Homegrown Division-sanctioned tournaments, leagues, showcases, Talent IDs, and camps run by PDev or Clubs, including local affiliate organizations (if the event is sanctioned by PDev). While PDev may not have authority or control over non-sanctioned events operated or organized by Clubs, any conduct prohibited by the Rules and Policies even at non-sanctioned events may serve as an independent basis for disciplinary action if it calls into question a Participant's fitness to participate in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events or MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competitions.

y. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division High School Waiver

The waiver form that must be completed if a registered Primary Player's admission or scholarship at a high school requires the Player's participation on the school's high school soccer team.

z. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Participant Agreement and Waiver

The waiver and release agreement that all Players (and, to the extent applicable, Players' parents/legal guardians), Club Representatives, or any other Participant must sign before they may participate in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division.

aa. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Showcases

MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competitions held nationally by PDev across select age groups. Unless otherwise stated by MLS NEXT, Club participation is optional at MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Showcases. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Showcases are part of the Regular Season.

bb. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Technical Standards

All rules and guidelines related to the technical soccer standards of any MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition as well as training standards. These include, but are not limited to, substitutions, field size, and coach licensing and certification.

ii. Post-Season

kk. Program Staff

ll. Protest

An objection of any kind related to a Match Decision.

mm. Registration

The process by which each Player, Club, and Club Representative submits required information and agrees to abide by the Rules and Policies. Registration is currently done online.

nn. Regular Season

The time period beginning on a date set by PDev (typically in September) and concluding on a date specified by PDev before the beginning of the MLS NEXT Cup during which MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition between Clubs occurs, inclusive of regional and winter MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Showcases, and counting towards points for regular season standings.

Collectively, these MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Rules and Regulations, along with each Club's Membership Agreement, the MLS NEXT Commercial Marketing Guidelines, the MLS NEXT Style Guide, the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Disciplinary Code, the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Code of Conduct, the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy, the MLS Privacy Policy, and any other rules, regulations, policies, guidelines, procedures, or directives of PDev, including, without limitation, any other organizational agreements of PDev and its affiliates (in each case, as they exist on the date hereof and as they may be amended, restated, supplemented, or otherwise modified from time to time by PDev).

uu. Team

Any grouping of Players by age by a Club to play in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competitions.

vv. USSF (the "Federation" or "U.S. Soccer")

The United States Soccer Federation, the official governing body of the sport of soccer in the United States.

III. SafeSport, Anti-Abuse, Anti-Discrimination, and Safety Compliance Framework

PDev is committed to making soccer the preeminent sport in the United States by serving our Players, Members, and their fans. The MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy, which incorporates policies mandated by federal law, the U.S. Center for SafeSport (the "Center"), and Canada Soccer's equivalent SafeSport policy (as promulgated by the Canadian Centre for Ethics in Sport ("CCES"), Sport Information Resource Centre ("SIRC"), and by Sport Canada), as well as the U.S. Soccer Federation, represents a foundational structure for building a soccer community where all participants, be they athletes, coaches, referees, parents, administrators, or volunteers, play an active role in creating a soccer atmosphere free of all forms of emotional, physical, and sexual misconduct.

In addition, the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy prohibits engaging in any conduct that amounts to unfair, adverse, discriminatory, or unequal treatment based on an individual's Protected Status, including, without limitation, racism, or sexism. PDev is committed to maintaining an environment at all MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events that is free from all forms of discrimination.

The MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy is incorporated into all Rules and Policies and can be found on the MLS NEXT Website. Failure of any Participant to comply with the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy or the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Code of Conduct will subject the non-compliant Club and/or Participant to discipline in accordance with the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Disciplinary Code and the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy.

IV. Governance and Organization

a. PDev's Authority & Discretion

MLS NEXT Homegrown Division is organized, managed, and administered by PDev. By signing the Membership Agreement, Clubs agree that the decision-making and policy-setting of PDev, including, but not limited to, setting forth the Rules and Policies, setting a competition schedule, and imposing a framework for issuing discipline, shall be made by PDev in its sole and absolute discretion.

b. Discipline

Any and all Infractions may subject a Club or a Participant to discipline under the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Disciplinary Code. Should a Club or Participant be found in violation of any of the Rules and Policies, penalties may vary based on the type and frequency of the Infraction and may result in fines, denied participation in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competitions and MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events, expulsion from the Program, among other potential penalties in PDev's sole and absolute discretion. The Clubs may bear the financial costs of any training mandated as a result of any rule violation. For more information, please see the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Disciplinary Code.

c. Committee Structure

PDev believes that the input, collaboration, and expertise of its Clubs will enhance its success. Therefore, PDev will create the Membership Advisory Committee ("MAC"), an ad hoc committee to receive input from its members in areas such as: competition, environment, individual growth, Player identification, commercial opportunities, and equity and inclusion. The MAC provides a forum for Participants to communicate their opinions, share their expertise, study issues, coordinate services, and develop recommendations. PDev will retain final decision-making authority but will consider recommendations presented by the MAC.

PDev may create additional Working Groups during the course of the 2025–2026 Season, or future seasons, to ensure engagement and collaboration with key soccer stakeholders in the U.S. and Canada.

1. MAC Objectives

The MAC shall be responsible for the following:

  • Facilitate collaboration, cooperation, and understanding between PDev and Participants so that PDev may react faster to Participants' interests, priorities, and concerns.
  • The MAC will provide a forum for Participants to communicate their opinions, share their expertise, study issues, coordinate services, and develop recommendations. PDev shall rotate MAC members every two years.

2. MAC Membership

The MLS NEXT General Manager is the MAC Chairman. The Chairman will work with and receive nominations from Participants for membership on the MAC. The Chairman shall have sole and absolute authority to appoint members to the MAC. The MAC shall consist of at least twelve (12) members, comprising of both MLS Academy and non-MLS Academy Club representation. The membership of the MAC shall rotate every two years.

3. MAC Meetings

The MAC shall meet with such frequency and at such intervals as the Chairman determines is necessary to carry out the MAC's duties and responsibilities but will plan to meet on a quarterly cadence. The MAC may permit attendance at meetings by Club management or other members as the MAC determines appropriate or advisable from time to time.

4. MAC Goals

The MAC shall establish goals for the following year at the last annual meeting. The MAC shall present its written goals to all Participants on an annual basis. In addition, at the end of each two-year cycle, the MAC shall present a written report detailing the MAC's actions in satisfaction of its stated goals and objectives.

V. MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Membership

a. Consideration For Membership

Each Club that wishes to participate in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division must submit a completed application prior to the application deadline determined by PDev to be considered for admission into the Program. The precise criteria that will be applied in assessing applicant clubs will be determined by PDev in its sole and absolute discretion. However, it is expected that the following factors, among others, may be considered, with the weight of each factor to be decided by PDev in its sole and absolute discretion:

  1. Club philosophy
  2. Leadership and governance
  3. History of elite player development
  4. Strength of current and potential player pool
  5. Expertise and credentials of coaches and technical staff
  6. Daily performance environment, including training, matches, facilities, staff support, non-soccer programs, etc.
  7. Ability to reduce or eliminate costs for players
  8. Geography
  9. Disciplinary history (including criminal or Safety and Wellbeing violations)
  10. Evidence of non-discriminatory, inclusive policies, processes and practices

The Program Staff will aim to review and evaluate each application every calendar year. Once the Program Staff has reviewed the submitted applications, the Program Staff will conduct live (virtual) interviews with clubs under consideration and gather feedback from local and national references.

PDev will make the final determination regarding which clubs are accepted and offered the opportunity to become Members of MLS NEXT Homegrown Division. An accepted Club will not become a Member until the Club has agreed to abide by all Rules and Policies, provided the required performance-bond payment to PDev, and entered into a fully-executed Membership Agreement.

b. Maintaining Eligibility

To remain in good standing, Clubs must be in full compliance with the Rules and Policies. In addition, PDev reserves the right to conduct evaluations of Clubs at any time; such evaluations may include review of both technical and administrative criteria (including as set forth above in Section V(a)), as determined by PDev in its sole discretion, as well as a review of the Club's compliance with the Rules and Policies.

c. Change of Ownership

A Club must give PDev written notice if the Club wants to change ownership, merge with another Club, change the location of the Club, or change the structure of the Club. PDev reserves the right to deny the Club's request, conduct a review of the Club prior to any such change, or determine whether the Club will remain in good standing. If PDev determines that a Club is not in compliance with such criteria or any of the Rules and Policies, the Club may be subject to discipline, up to and including removal from MLS NEXT Homegrown Division at PDev's sole discretion.

d. Withdrawal of a Club

In the event a Club withdraws from MLS NEXT Homegrown Division without finishing its scheduled Season, the Club shall forfeit its Membership and performance bond, and the following will apply:

  • If at least half of the full schedule of Matches for the Club in question has been played, all resulting scores stand as validated by PDev;
  • If less than half of the full schedule of Matches for the Club in question has been played, these completed Matches will be deleted from the standings; and
  • In the sole and absolute discretion of PDev, any remaining Matches shall either be recorded as a Forfeit victory for the opponent and/or rescheduled, as may be appropriate.

e. Termination

Pursuant to the Membership Agreement, PDev reserves the right to terminate any Club's Membership at any time for failure to meet MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Technical Standards, violation of any of the Rules and Policies, or taking or failing to stop any action that frustrates the purpose or intent of any Rules and Policies, in each case such determination shall be in accordance with the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Disciplinary Code.

VI. Club Non-Financial Responsibilities

a. Compliance with Rules and Policies

All Clubs and Participants are required to comply with the Rules and Policies. In order to comply, Clubs must adopt the Rules and Policies on behalf of their employees and all other Club Representatives. PDev reserves the right to decide how to enforce the Rules and Policies, in its sole and absolute discretion. Should a Club or any Participant associated with that Club be found in violation of any of the Rules and Policies, the Club's standing and Membership in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division, or the individual's participation in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division, may be jeopardized, as further described below.

In addition, the Rules and Policies are subject to change from time to time. Although PDev makes every effort not to modify the Rules and Policies mid-season, PDev reserves the right, in its sole and absolute discretion, to amend or enact new policies at any time, particularly to the extent required by applicable law, recommended by the U.S. Olympic and Paralympic Committee or the Center, or otherwise where PDev believes it is in the best interest of MLS NEXT Homegrown Division and/or the safety of its Players.

Subject to any applicable laws, PDev may use any information in determining disqualifications for participation in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division in PDev's absolute discretion. Such information may include, without limitation, conviction of any felony, gross misdemeanors, violation of Rules and Policies, or driving offenses, including, but not limited to: loss of license, reckless driving, impaired driving, or a DUI.

Any person accused of physical or sexual misconduct or otherwise violating the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy may be terminated, suspended, or placed in an alternate position by PDev until the matter is resolved. A person who is accused, but later cleared, of charges/allegations may apply to be reinstated in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division, which decision shall be in PDev's sole and absolute discretion. Reinstatement is not a right, and no guarantee is made that they will be reinstated to their former position or any other position within the MLS NEXT ecosystem.

b. Reporting Obligations

All Clubs and Covered Persons must comply with all reporting obligations set forth in the Rules and Policies. Failure by Clubs or Covered Persons to promptly report violations of the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy and SafeSport Code constitutes a violation of the Rules and Policies. Clubs, Covered Persons, and all Participants should report pertinent situations that appear to be in non-compliance with the Rules and Policies to PDev. Reporting non-compliance on the part of other Clubs should not be viewed as a negative, but rather as a positive attempt to maintain consistent standards that ensure professionalism, safety, and fairness, both on and off the field.

c. Conduct

Each Club must, upon PDev's renewal of a Club's membership in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division, execute and deliver its annual Membership Agreement to PDev prior to the start of the upcoming season. In the event a Club does not renew its Membership by executing the annual Membership Agreement and paying the performance bond as directed by PDev, such Club will not be permitted to participate in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events for that year. In the event a Club does not provide adequate notice (at least thirty (30) days) of such non-renewal, the Club will forfeit its performance bond. Each Club shall be responsible for the conduct of all its Participants, including, but not limited to, its Club Representatives, Players, parents, volunteers, and spectators.

d. Development

Heading:

  • Players aged 10 and younger are prohibited from heading the ball regardless of the age group in which they play.
  • Players who are 11, 12, and 13, regardless of the age group in which they play, may head the ball during Matches; however, these players must limit heading practice to a maximum of 30 minutes per week with no more than 15–20 headers per player, per week.

e. Scholarships

Requirements:

  • Each Club in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division is required to issue a minimum of one (1) full scholarship equivalent per season.
  • The full scholarship equivalent must be a financial amount equal to the cost of participating on an MLS NEXT Homegrown Division team, excluding travel (i.e., cost of tuition).
    • The financial support that results in a full scholarship equivalent may be issued across multiple families (e.g., instead of providing a single player/family one (1) full scholarship, a Club may issue financial assistance to four (4) families that is equal to 25% of a full scholarship).

Recommendations (not required):

  • Club financial support to families is "need-based."
  • Clubs issue financial support across several age groups.

VII. Outside Competition

a. Permissible Outside Competition

Subject to approval by PDev (and in the event of international tournament participation, additional PDev, USSF, and/or Canada Soccer approvals may be required), Clubs may participate in elite tournaments outside of the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Program. Clubs will only be permitted to participate in approved competitions and events that follow the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Technical Standards and are in compliance with the standards outlined in the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Outside Competition Standards (available in the Outside Competition Form). Clubs must receive prior approval from PDev for all elite tournaments outside of the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Program each Season. Club participation in outside competition without prior approval may result in discipline.

To request permission, the Club must submit to PDev an MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Outside Competition Application, including a roster and all necessary background information, no later than eight (8) weeks before the event. In the event the Team plans to travel internationally, the Club must also submit the appropriate documentation to the USSF (if the Club is based in the U.S.) or CSA (if the Club is based in Canada).

b. Player Participation in Outside Competitions

Players must meet all attendance and participation requirements under the Rules and Policies and under any individual Club requirements with respect to training and Matches for their Club during the Season. Absent extenuating circumstances (to be determined by PDev and the Club), the failure to meet those requirements should result in the Player's removal from the Club. PDev strongly encourages Clubs to ensure that Players meet the attendance and participation requirements—Clubs that do not take appropriate action to ensure that such attendance and participation requirements are met may be subject to Sanctions.

Clubs may not permit any Primary Player to participate in non-MLS NEXT Homegrown Division soccer activities during the Season, except as set forth in this Section and in connection with a Player's National Team duty and/or Youth National Team Identification Center participation. Clubs will be held accountable for the actions of their Players and Club Representatives, as well as the Club's parents, spectators, and volunteers. Players will be subject to discipline by both their own Club and PDev.

Future Players are able to participate in outside soccer leagues, as long as they are participating with their Club. Future Players cannot participate in outside soccer leagues with any other club.

c. Player Participation in Middle and High School Soccer

Primary Players must agree to forgo participating simultaneously in both (1) MLS NEXT Homegrown Division and (2) high school or middle school soccer.

Except as set forth herein, Primary Players who participate in high/middle school soccer are ineligible to participate in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events.

Only Primary Players who have been granted a High School Waiver by PDev will be allowed to remain on their Club's Roster during the high school soccer season (waivers are not available for middle school). Those Primary Players are permitted to participate in both high school soccer and MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition simultaneously during this period.

This waiver must be granted by PDev during one of the High School Waiver Windows to be considered for the Roster exemption for the 2025–2026 MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Season. The waiver eligibility guidelines are outlined in the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division High School Waiver. The High School Waiver Windows are set forth below:

  • Fall and Winter high school soccer season participants must submit the High School Waiver no later than September 9, 2025.
  • Spring high school soccer season participants must submit the High School Waiver between January 1, 2026, and February 13, 2026.

Although Clubs are not limited to a specific number of waivers, the number is naturally self-limiting to field a full Team throughout the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Season.

If a waiver is granted, the Primary Player must comply with the disclosure requirement and secure a full injury and disciplinary report from their high school team to provide to their Club.

All Primary Players who do not meet waiver eligibility and choose to participate with their high school soccer team during the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Season should not be registered for MLS NEXT Homegrown Division during that Fall, Winter or Spring high school season period. If a Club wishes to add the Primary Player to the roster at the conclusion of that Player's high school soccer season, that will count toward the Club's player adds and the Primary Player may enter the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Season as of the next Semester.

Future Players are permitted to participate simultaneously in both (1) MLS NEXT Homegrown Division and (2) high school soccer.

d. College Camps

Players will be permitted to attend winter college camp, provided that the Coach has approved attendance in advance. The following guidelines are in place for college camps:

  • The camp must be hosted by an NCAA institution in accordance with NCAA regulations;
  • The camp may be no longer than three (3) days in length;
  • The Player may only attend one (1) camp per school per year; and
  • The Player may not attend a camp if it falls during the Competitive Season or is equal to or less than one (1) week prior to the start of the Competitive Season.

Summer college camps that fall outside of the Season do not apply and Players may attend such camps without needing to request a waiver.

No insurance coverage provided by PDev will cover any occurrences arising out of attendance in Non-MLS NEXT Homegrown Division activities, including, but not limited to, winter or summer college camps, tryouts, or other competitions.

VIII. Expectations of Clubs

a. Training and Match Expectations

All Clubs are required to complete their entire Regular Season schedule and to attend all MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Events scheduled for their age groups. As further detailed in the Membership Agreement, failure to field a Team for a scheduled Match resulting in a Forfeit will cause the Club to be fined. All Forfeits are subject to additional fines and disciplinary action at PDev's sole and absolute discretion. Any Club forfeiting a Match due to failing to field a Team during its membership in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division may be removed from the Program in PDev's sole and absolute discretion.

Clubs are responsible for fielding teams that provide meaningful competition for other Members. In the event a Club is not able to field consistently competitive teams in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division, PDev will notify the Club of the concern and work in good faith on solutions and timelines to improve the Club's competitiveness. In the event the Club is unable to become more competitive, such that the Club is providing meaningful Matches for other Members, the Club may be subject to removal from the Program.

Clubs are responsible for adhering to all Match Day policies and procedures outlined and explained in the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Match Day Guide. Clubs should make efforts to acquire the best facilities possible as outlined below. Clubs must train and play home Matches within reasonable geographic proximity to their Club's home location unless approved by PDev.

b. Inclusion & Non-Discrimination

MLS NEXT Homegrown Division is an inclusive and non-discriminatory Program. PDev does not and will not tolerate any racist, discriminatory or inappropriately exclusive behavior by any Participant, including any Club.

PDev has set out to create an equitable and inclusive development environment. To that end, Clubs shall make all efforts to establish a positive and safe environment for all Participants, without regard to race, ethnicity, income level, culture, sexual orientation, and gender. As outlined in the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy, it is recommended that each Club provide inclusion and awareness training for its Participants. To promote a positive and inclusive culture, Clubs should use best efforts to provide training on an annual basis and ensure that their Players and Club Representatives complete such training prior to the beginning of the Regular Season (and more frequently if needed).

c. Non-Technical Facility Requirements

Clubs should acquire Facilities that (if feasible): (1) have separate locker rooms for Players on different teams; (2) have separate and functionally equivalent changing areas for different genders, as well as separate restrooms for Adults and Minors; and (3) the field must be designed for soccer use and adequately maintained and repaired. In addition, prior to the use of a field, the field must be inspected as set forth in the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy.

d. Mandatory Meeting Attendance

The Club must send at least one (1) Club director and one (1) Club administrator to each mandatory MLS NEXT Homegrown Division meeting or conference call as scheduled by PDev, unless otherwise notified.

e. Recruiting Players/Territories

No Club has exclusive territory rights. Clubs may recruit Players for participation on Clubs from any geographic region. a. Eligible Players

To be considered an eligible Player, any Player listed on any Club Roster must meet the age requirement for their Club, have completed the registration process outlined in Section XI(c), have paid the registration fee, and must not be subject to a Suspension (to serve a suspension, a Player is required to sit for the entire subsequent match day; the Player is not eligible to play with an older age group on the day the Player serves the suspension).

Each Club's Roster may have a maximum of twenty-eight (28) Players per age group and a minimum of 14 Primary Players per age group. There is no limit to the number of Rostered Players that do not have United States citizenship.

iii. Primary Player Adds

Clubs will have one period during the season (August 22, 2025, through April 16, 2026) at which time Players may be added as Primary Player (either adding a new Primary Player or moving a Future Player to Primary Player status).

The Club will have a total of six (6) Primary Player Adds per MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Team with the discretion to allocate the Player Adds across their MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Teams as they see fit. The total number of Primary Player adds should not exceed the number of MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Teams multiplied by six (6) and can only be utilized during this window. For example, an MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Club with only U15 and U17 teams can add up to, but not exceed, twelve (12) total Players across the two teams.

Clubs that pass a Player on to a Club with an MLS professional pathway will not be charged with using a Player Add to replace that individual. In addition, Clubs that promote a Player on to an MLS professional team in that Club's pathway in good faith will not be charged with using a Player Add to replace that individual and will be permitted an extra roster spot in the event that the promoted Player returns to the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Team.

Any unused Player Adds cannot be utilized beyond the deadline unless a Club is promoting a Future Player (added before the deadline) to Primary Player after the Player Add window and has an open Roster slot.

iv. Injured Players

If a Club is operating at the Roster maximum, it may replace a Player with a serious injury (as supported by written documentation from a medical professional detailing the injury) with a new Player in their Roster spot. If the injured Player is medically cleared to return during the Season, the Club may allow him/her/them to replace the Player that took his/her/their Roster spot with no deduction of Player Adds.

v. Player Movement

Please refer to MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Player Movement Guidelines.

vi. Future Players

Clubs may add Future Players across all of a Club's age groups (U13 through U19), which will count against Roster totals, but will not count as a Primary Add.

A Future Player is a Player who is Rostered with a Club who is not ready to contribute at the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division level full time. A Future Player is not playing consistently and/or playing enough minutes in Matches to fully develop; therefore, the Player is eligible to play matches outside of MLS NEXT Homegrown Division with the Club with which they are registered. A Future Player is required to train a minimum of 1x per week with the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Team that registered them as a Future Player and overall should consistently train 3-4 times per week (depending on the age group).

A Future Player can play in a maximum of seven (7) Matches in a Season. Note, at U13 and U14 age groups, any Future Player added to the Match Day Roster will count as having played in a Match. To supplement his/her/their Match minutes, a Future Player is eligible to also play matches outside MLS NEXT Homegrown Division with a non-MLS NEXT Homegrown Division team within the Club as part of his/her/their individual development plan. A Future Player must still follow the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division play-to-rest ratio.

A Future Player may not be registered with two different Clubs at the same time during the same Season. A Future Player may only change his/her/their status to Primary once during the Season and may not return to Future Player status in the same Season.

FIFA regulations require PDev to obtain appropriate clearances on behalf of these Players before they can register and participate in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division.

XIII. Competition

a. Overview

PDev will place Clubs into geographically-based Conferences and set a schedule. PDev reserves the right to adjust Match scheduling.

b. Schedule

PDev will create and manage the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division competition schedule, inclusive of the Post-Season. The MLS NEXT Cup typically will be held at the end of the Regular Season. If a Club would like to change a Fixture date, it must contact PDev four (4) weeks in advance of the Fixture date. All changes are subject to the approval of PDev.

c. Venue/Kick-off Times/Turnaround Time

PDev shall set the dates of all MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competitions. The venues and specific kick-off times of the Matches shall be proposed by the home Team, subject to the approval of PDev. The home Team shall submit venues and specific kick-off times to PDev for all Matches by a date determined each year by Program Staff, so visiting Teams can make the appropriate arrangements or offer objections for PDev's consideration. All Clubs must provide a turf or synthetic alternative in the event of inclement weather. Evening Matches may only be played at venues with appropriate lighting installations.

  • Double Fixture weekends may be played; however, a minimum of eighteen (18) hours must be provided between both kick-off times to allow for necessary travel and recovery. For instance, if a Saturday Fixture kick-off is 6:00 pm local time, the Sunday Fixture may not kick-off earlier than 12:00 pm local time unless authorized by PDev.
  • Clubs may not request to schedule three (3) Matches in four (4) days during Regular Season competition.
  • Matches may not kick off earlier than 9:00 am local time without permission from Program Staff.
  • When scheduling home Matches, the home Team determines kick-off order. PDev recommends that Matches be played from oldest to youngest when possible.
  • When scheduling multiple Matches on the same field, please allow for at least thirty (30) minutes of warm-up time prior to each kickoff. Less than thirty (30) minutes between each match is permitted if there is adequate warm-up space on an adjacent field.
  • Moving a Fixture venue or kickoff time for any reason within fourteen (14) days of the scheduled Fixture may result in fines and/or Sanctions.

d. Facility Set-Up

The home Team shall be responsible for setting up its venue to include the appropriate lines, goals, nets, corner flags, benches, and water set up for both Teams and ensuring a clean and well-maintained field in accordance with the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Safety and Wellbeing Policy. e. Abandonment

In serious cases, the Club may also be disqualified from the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition in progress and its Membership may be revoked.

All postponements and cancellations for any reason other than the health and safety of Participants must be approved by PDev. A Club may postpone any Match in the event the Club believes in good faith that proceeding with a Match would pose a significant risk to the health and safety of any Participants.

If there is no rescheduled date within seven (7) days after the postponed Fixture, PDev will choose the date, location, and time for the rescheduled Match and each Club will be required to participate, subject to any health and safety guidelines released by PDev. l. Conference Tiebreakers

If two (2) or more Clubs finish the Regular Season with an equal number of Points per Match the following procedure (in order of priority) will be used to break the tie and determine the final standings ("Regular Season Tiebreaker"):

  1. Head-to-head (only applicable to tie of two (2) Clubs)
  2. Number of Wins per Match
  3. Goal differential per Match
  4. Goals for per Match
  5. Away goal differential per Match
  6. Away goals for per Match
  7. Home goal differential per Match
  8. Home goals for per Match
  9. Fewest Disciplinary points:
    • One (1) point for every Yellow Card received
    • Three (3) points for every Red Card received
    • Five (5) points for any ejections of staff or Players from the technical area
  10. Coin toss (tie of two (2) Clubs) or drawing of lots (tie of three (3) or more Clubs)

m. Wildcard Tiebreakers

Wildcard tiebreakers shall be determined in the same manner as Conference tiebreakers.

XIV. Matches, Laws of the Game, Disciplinary Matters, Disputes, Protests, and Appeals

a. Laws of the Game

Unless otherwise provided in the Rules and Policies, all Matches shall be played in accordance with the Laws of the Game in force at the time of the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Competition and as promulgated by the International Football Association Board. In the case of any discrepancy in the interpretation of the Laws of the Game, the English version shall be authoritative.

b. Disciplinary Matters

  • Incidents and events requiring disciplinary action are handled in compliance with the MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Disciplinary Code. The Players and Clubs participating in MLS NEXT Homegrown Division agree to comply with the Laws of the Game and all Rules and Policies.
  • Any Coach/Club Representative who has received a Match Suspension cannot be present at the Club facilities (including the soccer fields or Club offices) and cannot attend any Club-approved activity including, but not limited to, a Match, MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Tournament, or other MLS NEXT Homegrown Division Event during the pendency of the Sanction. Any suspended Coach/Club Representative who is found to have violated this prohibition will receive an additional Match Suspension of two (2) Fixtures.
  • Reporting Egregious Fouls After the Fact: Clubs have 72 hours following a Match to report any egregious in-match fouls, not acknowledged by the referee, to PDev after noticing the egregious foul during film review.

c. Protests of Officiating Decisions

If a Match Decision is subject to Protest, it shall be submitted to the jurisdiction of PDev.

  • Time: Protests shall be submitted in writing to PDev within twelve (12) hours of the conclusion of the Match in question and followed up immediately with a full written report.
  • Eligibility: Protests regarding the eligibility of Matches shall be submitted in writing to PDev no later than 24 hours after the Match.
  • Field Conditions: Protests regarding the state of the field, its surroundings, markings, or accessory items shall be made to the referee before the start of the Match.
  • After a Match: Protests against any incidents that occur during the course of a Match shall be made to PDev immediately following the completion of the Match.
  • Referee's Decisions: No Protests may be made about the referee's decisions regarding facts connected with play; such decisions are final.
  • Frivolous Protests: If an unfounded or irresponsible Protest is lodged, PDev may dismiss the Protest.

d. Match Integrity Policy
