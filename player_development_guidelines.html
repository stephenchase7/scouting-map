Overview

These MLS Player Development Guidelines (these Guidelines) detail the provisions governing Player Development Programs and MLS Homegrown Players. These Guidelines reference relevant sections of the MLS Roster and Budget Guidelines (as amended and restated from time to time, the Roster and Budget Guidelines), the MLS Club Commercial Guidelines (as amended and restated from time to time, the Club Commercial Guidelines), and MLS NEXT Protocols (as amended and restated from time to time). Capitalized terms not otherwise defined in these Guidelines have the meanings ascribed to them in the MLS Roster and Budget Guidelines. Any section or part references made, refer to the relevant section or part of these Guidelines, unless noted otherwise. Where these Guidelines and MLS NEXT Protocols conflict with each other, the provisions of these Guidelines prevail.

These Guidelines are hereby incorporated in, and are deemed a part of, the League Rules.

The League reserves the right at any time to modify these Guidelines and to establish exceptions to these Guidelines in its sole and absolute discretion.

2. Non-Homegrown Territories

Any area of the United States not considered a Homegrown Territory is a Non-Homegrown Territory. Player Development Programs are limited to an MLS Club's Homegrown Territory, except for Non-Homegrown Territory Player Development Programs (as described in Section 3.2).

C. Player Development Programming

3. Player Development Program

An MLS Club must establish a Player Development Program. There are two types of Player Development Programs: (1) Homegrown Territory Player Programs and (2) Non-Homegrown Territory Player Programs.

3.1. Homegrown Territory Player Programs

3.1.1. Academy

An MLS Club may create an Academy within its Homegrown Territory for elite youth soccer talent to develop their skills and to represent the MLS Club in youth soccer competition (e.g., MLS NEXT). An MLS Club's Academy must always comply with these Guidelines, MLS NEXT Protocols, and any related League Rules.

In addition, if an MLS Club's Academy offers a residency component, then the Academy must provide and/or facilitate full-time education through high school.

3.1.2. Youth Affiliates

An MLS Club may form any number of youth affiliates with local amateur youth clubs within the MLS Club's Homegrown Territory that provide mutually agreed upon rights and benefits from MLS, an MLS Club, and/or adidas. Before finalizing an affiliation, the MLS Club must seek authorization from the League.

3.1.3. Other Programming

If an MLS Club offers other player development programming, including but not limited to talent identification camps, local youth tournaments, and soccer clinics, then the MLS Club must hold the programming within its Homegrown Territory.

3.2. Non-Homegrown Territory Player Programs

3.2.1. Up to Two Non-Homegrown Territory Player Development Programs

Subject to the League's prior written approval, an MLS Club may form up to two (2) owned and operated youth clubs outside of its Homegrown Territory, provided that the youth club:

  • is located within the United States or Canada;
  • is not located in another MLS Club's Home Territory or Homegrown Territory; and
  • is fully owned and operated by the MLS Club (e.g., the coaches and training environment must be under the direct supervision and control of the MLS Club).

Once a Non-Homegrown Territory Player Development Program is designated, the Club may only change the designation to a different program after providing twenty-four (24) months written notice to the League. The designation change is subject to the League's written approval.

3.2.2. All Other Programs or Partnerships Are Not Permitted

Other than Non-Homegrown Territory Player Development Programs, an MLS Club must not form any program or partnership outside its Homegrown Territory (regardless of whether it is for player development, marketing, or branding purposes). To the extent these relationships exist, the League may terminate these relationships. The League reserves the right to establish League Rules governing such relationships.

Commentary: An MLS Club must not employ or contract any individual who is affiliated with a youth soccer program outside its Homegrown Territory, as this would violate Section 3.2.2 above.

4. Player Development Program Requirements

If an MLS Club establishes an Academy, then the MLS Club must provide at least the following.

4.1. Rules and Regulations

4.1.1. Academy Player Handbook

If an MLS Club establishes an Academy, then the MLS Club must maintain a document that provides information specific to the MLS Club's Academy, including, but not limited to, Club contact information, Club rules/policies, the Homegrown Information Sheet, the Academy Acknowledgment Letter, and any other relevant information.

An MLS Club must distribute the Academy Player Handbook to players participating in the MLS Club's Academy annually.

4.1.2. FIFA Regulations on the Status and Transfer of Players ("FIFA RSTP")

An MLS Club must ensure that its Player Development Program complies with the FIFA RSTP, including, but not limited to, Article 19 ("Protection of Minors").

Please refer to Exhibit 5 for League guidance regarding MLS Club entitlement to Training Compensation under the FIFA RSTP for players trained in its Academy.

4.1.3. Acknowledgment of Player Obligations Memo

An MLS Club must ensure that all Academy players and their parent(s) or legal guardian(s) review and sign an acknowledgment form that provides information about the MLS Club's Player Development Program and its mission, details player obligations given the player's affiliation with the MLS Club, and explains that the MLS Club intends to preserve the player's NCAA eligibility unless and until the player agrees to a professional contract. The MLS Club must maintain a copy of each Academy player's signed Acknowledgement of Player Obligations Memo in its records.

Please refer to Exhibit 1 for the Acknowledgment of Player Obligations Memo.

4.1.4. Homegrown Information Sheet

An MLS Club must ensure that all Academy players and their parent(s) or legal guardian(s) review and sign an information sheet summarizing the current Homegrown Player criteria and requirements. The MLS Club must maintain a copy of each Academy player's signed Homegrown Information Sheet in its records.

Please refer to Exhibit 2 for the Homegrown Information Sheet.

4.1.5. NCAA Eligibility

An MLS Club must ensure that its Player Development Program does not jeopardize participating players' NCAA eligibility.

4.1.6. adidas Compliance

An MLS Club must ensure that all Academy coaches, staff, and players always wear adidas apparel and footwear and use MLS match balls when representing their MLS Club (e.g., games, training, team travel), except that players who have signed individual endorsement contracts with a different apparel company may wear different apparel products during these activities. An MLS Club must ensure that it manages its adidas product allotment so that adidas products are always available to all participating coaches, staff, and players. Noncompliance is subject to League-imposed discipline, including fines.

4.1.7. MLS NEXT Rules and Regulations

An MLS Club participating in MLS NEXT must follow all MLS NEXT Protocols, including the MLS NEXT Safety and Wellbeing Policy for MLS Academy players.

4.2. Training and Competition

4.2.1. MLS NEXT Seasons

An MLS Club must align its Player Development Program seasons with MLS NEXT Seasons.

4.2.2. Attendance Logs

An MLS Club must maintain attendance logs for each participating player in its Player Development Program. The League may request these attendance logs, including those pertaining to training sessions, at any time.

4.2.3. Youth Tournaments

An MLS Club must participate in youth soccer tournaments if instructed by the League (e.g., Generation adidas Cup).

D. Homegrown Players: Overview and Homegrown Priority

The rules governing prospective MLS Homegrown Players aim to incentivize MLS Clubs to develop top domestic talent within MLS Academies, while accounting for variations across the geographies of the MLS ecosystem.

5. Homegrown Framework

5.1. Homegrown Priority

Homegrown Priority is an entrance mechanism by which an MLS Club may sign an eligible player to a professional contract and add the player directly to the MLS Club's first-team roster with the Homegrown Player designation.

5.2. Homegrown Exclusivity

Homegrown Exclusivity is an exclusive right held by an MLS Club to develop (without undue influence from another MLS Club) for purposes of establishing Homegrown Priority up to forty (40) players across three (3) age groups and certain former Registered Academy Players who have gone to college or signed a professional contract with the MLS Club's MLS NEXT Pro team.

Homegrown Exclusivity is not Bona Fide Consideration as defined in the Roster and Budget Guidelines.

5.3. Homegrown Player List

The Homegrown Player List is the mechanism by which an MLS Club formally documents its Homegrown Exclusivity over qualifying players.

6. Homegrown Priority

An MLS Club establishes priority to sign a player to an MLS Standard Player Agreement (or an "MLS SPA") and to add the player to its first-team roster with the Homegrown Player designation after he completes twelve (12) continuous months registered with an MLS Club's Academy and/or an MLS Club's MLS NEXT Pro team as a Registered Academy Player. The end of the twelve (12)-month period must fall between January 31 of the year in which the player turns fourteen (14) and the year in which he turns eighteen (18).

7. Homegrown Player

To add a player to an MLS Club's first-team roster as a Homegrown Player, the MLS Club must comply with the following:

  • Have established Homegrown Priority and maintained Homegrown Exclusivity;
  • Submit to the League a Homegrown Player Request Form via MLS Connect; and
  • Engage with the League Office before agreeing to any terms with a potential Homegrown Player and/or his representative(s).

Upon receiving League approval, an MLS Club may add a Homegrown Player to its first-team roster.

Commentary: Upon receipt of the Homegrown Player Request Form, the League Office will determine, in its sole and absolute discretion, whether the MLS Club has Homegrown Priority and Homegrown Exclusivity over the player. If approved by the League, the Homegrown Player will not be subject to the MLS SuperDraft or any other central entrance mechanism. There is no limit on the number of Homegrown Players an MLS Club may add to its first-team roster.

Page 13

Commentary: For an MLS Club to achieve Homegrown Priority over a player by January 31 of the year the player turns fourteen (14), the player must: (a) be a Registered Academy Player with the MLS Club on such date, and (b) have been registered with the MLS Club for twelve (12) continuous months prior to January 31 of the year the player turns fourteen (14).

Commentary: An MLS Club may sign a player over whom the MLS Club has not yet achieved Homegrown Priority to an MLS SPA with the intention of adding the player to its MLS first-team roster as a Homegrown Player. In this case, the MLS Club would wait until the MLS Club achieves Homegrown Priority over the player before adding the player to the MLS Club's first-team roster (i.e., twelve continuous months registered with the MLS Club's Academy and/or the MLS Club's MLS NEXT Pro team).

Commentary: An MLS Club may trade for Homegrown Priority for a player over whom a different MLS Club has achieved Homegrown Priority. As a result of the trade, the player will be considered a Homegrown Player for the acquiring MLS Club if he signs an MLS SPA.

Commentary: An MLS Club may trade Homegrown Priority over a player but not Homegrown Exclusivity over a player nor rights to Underage Players within its Homegrown Territory. For example, if a player is listed on the MLS Club's Homegrown Player List but the MLS Club has not achieved Homegrown Priority over the player, then the MLS Club does not have Bona Fide Consideration to trade related to the player. If the player wants to join a different MLS Club's Academy, his current and future MLS Clubs may agree to a settlement whereby, for example, the Current Club waives the right to bring a tampering charge or agrees to remove the player from its Plus 5. MLS Clubs may negotiate a settlement among themselves and later submit the settlement for League review and approval.

Commentary: The League may, in its sole and absolute discretion, continue to sign any top young player to Generation adidas for the MLS SuperDraft irrespective of his placement on an MLS Club's Homegrown Player List.

E. Homegrown Exclusivity

With the intention of establishing Homegrown Priority, an MLS Club holds the exclusive right to develop players who meet the qualifications listed below (without undue influence from another MLS Club). A player over whom an MLS Club maintains Homegrown Exclusivity, by including the player on its Homegrown Player List, is ineligible to be recruited by other MLS Clubs.

8. Homegrown Exclusivity - Eligibility

8.1. "The 35" Registered Academy Players

An MLS Club may establish Homegrown Exclusivity over up to thirty-five (35) Registered Academy Players between its U15, U17, and U19 age groups. For the U15 and U17 age groups respectively, an MLS Club may include no more than twenty (20) players. For the U19 age group, an MLS Club may include no more than fifteen (15) players.

8.1.1.

Homegrown Exclusivity over a Registered Academy Player expires when the player departs the MLS Club's Academy.

a. Extending Homegrown Exclusivity over a Departing Registered Academy Player

An MLS Club may extend Homegrown Exclusivity over the departing Registered Academy Player by including the player on its Homegrown Player List as a Plus 5 Player or Post-Formation Player, if otherwise eligible.

For a former Registered Academy Player who is not eligible to be included on the Homegrown Player List as a Post-Formation Player, an MLS Club may extend Homegrown Exclusivity over the player by including the player on its Homegrown Player List as a Plus 5 Player only if the MLS Club has already achieved Homegrown Priority over the player and the player meets the requirements of a Plus 5 Player except for 8.2.1.b.

b. Seeking a Right of First Refusal (ROFR) for a Departing Registered Academy Player

An MLS Club may seek a ROFR for the departing Registered Academy Player by requesting that the League makes, on behalf of the MLS Club, a Genuine Offer to the player for an MLS SPA or for an MLS NEXT Pro contract (as long as an MLS SPA is also offered and begins within twelve (12) months of the start date of the MLS NEXT Pro contract) by August 31 of the year that he turns eighteen (18) years old.

If the player declines the offer, then the MLS Club may submit a request for a ROFR through MLS Connect.

Commentary: See Figure E(A) below.

Commentary: Further to the Roster and Budget Guidelines, a Club will only be eligible to receive a ROFR if the League issues a written Genuine Offer to the player on behalf of the Club. Alternatively, the Club may issue the Genuine Offer only if the League previously approved the Genuine Offer and if the League is copied on all Club correspondence.

Commentary: Offering an MLS NEXT Pro contract alone does not provide an MLS Club the opportunity to be granted a ROFR. The MLS Club must also offer an MLS SPA that would begin within twelve (12) months of the start date of the MLS NEXT Pro contract.

8.2. "Plus 5" Players

8.2.1.

Additionally, an MLS Club may establish Homegrown Exclusivity over up to five (5) players who:

  • Are not Registered Academy Players;
  • Have a permanent address in or currently reside with their parent(s) or legal guardian(s) in the MLS Club's Homegrown Territory;
  • Are in one of the U15, U17, or U19 age groups. For each age group, an MLS Club may include up to five (5) players; and
  • Each of the five (5) players, the player's family, and the player's current associated soccer club have been notified in writing by the MLS Club of the player's inclusion as a Plus 5 player. In its written notification, the Club must also offer a position in the Club's Academy in the current season corresponding with the applicable Submission Date.
8.2.2.

Homegrown Exclusivity over a non-professional player Plus 5 player expires August 31 of the year the player turns eighteen (18) or upon his enrollment in college (whichever is sooner); thereafter, the player is no longer eligible for the Homegrown Player List.

8.2.3.

Homegrown Exclusivity over a professional player listed on an MLS Club's Plus 5 expires December 31 of the year that he turns seventeen (17) years old; thereafter, the player is no longer eligible for the Homegrown Player List. If the MLS Club does not sign the player to an MLS SPA or to a professional contract in MLS NEXT Pro by that date, the player will be subject to priority rules as outlined in the Roster and Budget Guidelines.

8.2.4.

If an MLS Club requests that the League makes, on behalf of the MLS Club, a Genuine Offer to a Plus 5 player for an MLS SPA or for an MLS NEXT Pro contract (as long as an MLS SPA is also offered and begins within twelve (12) months of the start date of the MLS NEXT Pro contract) by December 31 of the year that he turns seventeen (17) years old and if the player declines, then the MLS Club may submit a request for a ROFR through MLS Connect.

Commentary: See Figure E(B) below.

Commentary: Offering an MLS NEXT Pro contract alone does not provide an MLS Club the opportunity to be granted a ROFR.

Commentary: The term priority in Section 8.2.3 refers to the concept of priority detailed in the Roster and Budget Guidelines and not Homegrown Priority.

Commentary: An MLS Club must not include a Plus 5 Player on its Homegrown Player List who currently resides in a different MLS Club's Homegrown Territory or in a Non-Homegrown Territory.

8.3. Post-Formation Players: College and MLS NEXT Pro

8.3.1.

An MLS Club may extend Homegrown Exclusivity, for a limited time, over any former Registered Academy Player enrolled in college or signed to a professional contract with the MLS Club's MLS NEXT Pro team, if the MLS Club:

  • Already achieved Homegrown Priority over the player at the time of college enrollment or of signing a professional contract in MLS NEXT Pro; and
  • Properly included the player on its Homegrown Player List at the time of college enrollment or of signing a professional contract in MLS NEXT Pro.
Page 16
8.3.2.

If an MLS Club signs a player to a professional contract with MLS or the MLS Club's MLS NEXT Pro team who is otherwise eligible to be listed among its 35 Registered Academy Players, then the MLS Club may list the player among its Post-Formation players.

(8.3.1.-8.3.2. together, Post-Formation Players)

8.3.3.

For Post-Formation Players enrolled in college, Homegrown Exclusivity expires December 1 of the player's collegiate senior year (i.e., the player's fourth (4th) year of enrollment), at which point an MLS Club must offer an MLS SPA to sign the player as a Homegrown Player if the MLS Club wishes to remove the player from the MLS SuperDraft.

If a player is enrolled in college, an MLS Club need not offer the player a professional contract before December 1 of the player's collegiate senior year. But if the player leaves college early to pursue professional playing opportunities, then the MLS Club may request that the League makes, on behalf of the MLS Club, a Genuine Offer to the player for an MLS SPA or for an MLS NEXT Pro contract (as long as an MLS SPA is also offered and begins within twelve (12) months of the start date of the MLS NEXT Pro contract). The MLS Club will not retain priority if an MLS SPA is not offered.

If the player declines the offer, then the MLS Club may submit a request for a ROFR through MLS Connect.

8.3.4.

For Post-Formation Players signed with an MLS Club's MLS NEXT Pro team, Homegrown Exclusivity expires on the earlier of the expiration date of the MLS NEXT Pro contract or December 1 of the year the player turns twenty-two (22) years old.

Commentary: Section 8.3.2 addresses the scenario where an MLS Club recruits a player to join its Academy for the first time by signing the player to a professional contract. Ordinarily, the player would register with the Academy and the MLS Club would list the player among its 35 Registered Academy Players on the Homegrown Player List until the MLS Club achieves Homegrown Priority over the player to add the player to the MLS Club's first-team roster as a Homegrown Player. With this Section, an MLS Club may choose to list the player among its Post-Formation Players for this period rather than using a slot among The 35 to do so.

9. Recruiting

Subject to the rules below, an MLS Club may recruit eligible players to join its Academy. These rules only apply to MLS Clubs and their Academies (i.e., MLS Clubs recruiting eligible players from other MLS Clubs). MLS Clubs recruiting players from non-MLS MLS NEXT clubs must follow MLS NEXT Protocols.

9.1. Recruitable Players

If no MLS Club retains a ROFR or Homegrown Exclusivity over a player, based on the Homegrown Player List, then the player is eligible to be recruited by any MLS Club to register with its Academy subject to the below requirements (Recruitable Players).

Page 18

9.2. Underage Players

9.2.1. Underage Players – Ineligible to be Recruited

If a player is (or turns) thirteen (13) years old or younger in the year of these Guidelines and has a permanent address in or currently resides with their parent(s) or legal guardian(s) in an MLS Club's Homegrown Territory, then the player is not eligible to be recruited.

9.2.2. Shared Territories

MLS Clubs with overlapping Homegrown Territories may recruit and include in their respective Player Development Programming a youth player within their Homegrown Territory who is thirteen (13) years old or younger in the year of these Guidelines. Except that an MLS Club must not recruit a player who is registered with the shared territory MLS Club's Academy and competing within MLS NEXT competition.

Commentary: An MLS Club may recruit an Underage Player who has a permanent address in or currently resides with their parents(s) or legal guardian(s) in its own Homegrown Territory to join its Academy.

9.3. Remuneration for Recruitable Academy Players

9.3.1.

If an MLS Club interested in recruiting a Recruitable Player (the Recruiting Club) wants to register a Recruitable Academy Player (i.e., a type of Recruitable Player who is also a Registered Academy Player), then the Recruiting Club must provide Official Notification to the MLS Club with which the player is currently registered (the Current Club) (See Section 9.4 below). Upon the player registering with the Recruiting Club, the Current Club may request that the League instruct the Recruiting Club to provide to the Current Club the following:

  • $20,000 General Allocation Money payable upon the player signing an MLS SPA; and
  • Twenty Percent (20%) of any future transfer fee, loan fee, or training compensation received (regardless of the MLS Club with which the player is registered at the time of the transfer, loan, or registration as a professional) in the event the player transfers to a club outside MLS or of any Financial Consideration received if the player is traded for Financial Consideration within MLS.

Note, remuneration under this Section does not include Homegrown Priority, regardless of whether it has been achieved by the Current Club.

Notwithstanding the above, MLS Clubs may agree to different terms. If the MLS Clubs are unable to reach an agreement, then the League will impose the preset terms above. The one (1) year Statute of Limitations applies to all remuneration requests.

9.3.2.

All other Recruitable Players may be recruited and subsequently registered to a Recruiting Club with no remuneration immediately due to the player's former club or academy, save for any amounts owed in connection with the MLS NEXT Scholarship.

Commentary: The remuneration above is not applicable if the player remains a Recruitable Player upon the distribution of the Homegrown Player List following the player's departure from the Current Club.

9.4. Official Notification and Communication of Player Interest

9.4.1.

For a Recruitable Academy Player, a Recruiting Club must provide a written notification of interest for the player to his Current Club. The Recruiting Club must include the following information in the notice:

  • Player Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Request for Parent Contact Information
  • Request that the Club notify Parent(s) of the Recruiting Club's interest
  • Whether trial is requested and on what basis

Commentary: If the Recruiting Club is interested in recruiting more than one Recruitable Academy Player from the same MLS Club, then the Recruiting Club may aggregate its requests in one notification.

Page 19
9.4.2.

The Recruiting Club must not contact the Recruitable Academy Player or his family until it provides Official Notification and receives a response from the Current Club, as outlined in Section 9.4.3 below. The same rule applies to an MLS Club that receives unsolicited contact from a player who the MLS Club is interested in recruiting.

9.4.3.

The Current Club must notify the Recruitable Academy Player and his family of the Official Notification and the request for trial (if any) within three (3) calendar days of receiving the Official Notification. Within the same three (3)-day period, the Current Club must respond in writing to the Recruiting MLS Club with the following:

  • Player Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Confirmation that the player and his family have been notified
  • Consent to contact the player and the contact details of the player's family (alternatively, if the MLS Club does not grant consent, the basis for the decision (including that the family supports the decision))
  • Suggested dates for trial (including alternative dates), if applicable

Commentary: In the event the Current Club fails to respond in writing within three (3) days of receiving the Official Notification, the Recruiting Club may request via email intervention from the League.

9.5. Player Trials

A Recruiting Club may invite a Recruitable Academy Player to trial with the MLS Club's Academy. Subject to the rules below, if the Recruitable Academy Player accepts an invitation to trial with the MLS Club, then he and the Recruiting Club may together determine the details of the trial.

9.5.1. Mandatory Release Trial Windows

A Recruitable Academy Player may trial with an MLS Club's Academy during either of the two Mandatory Release Trial Windows below without consent of his current MLS Club. Although consent from the Current Club is not required, the Recruiting Club and Current Club must comply with the communication requirements above and are encouraged to collaborate to find trial dates that do not interfere with schedules of the Recruitable Academy Player and the involved MLS Clubs.

  • Spring Trial Window (February – March) – This window follows the Spring Homegrown Player List Submission
  • Fall Trial Window (September – December) – This window follows the Fall Homegrown Player List Submission

MLS Clubs recruiting a Recruitable Academy Player may request a trial outside of the Mandatory Release Trial Windows, but the Recruitable Player's current MLS Club may decline to release the Player.

Page 20
9.5.2. Trial Structure Requirements

A Recruitable Academy Player on trial with an MLS Club's Academy (a Trialist) may trial for up to seven (7) days, so long as the trial does not unreasonably interfere with the Trialist's youth competition matches with the Current Club. The Recruiting Club must not include a Trialist in its official competition matches (e.g., MLS NEXT).

9.6. Academy Commitment Letters

A Recruiting Club may enter a commitment letter with a Recruitable Player who intends to register with the MLS Club's Academy but wants to reserve a position in the Academy until the end of the current school year, subject to the below (an Academy Commitment Letter). The Recruiting Club must return a fully executed Academy Commitment Letter for League review and approval. Thereafter, the League will notify all MLS Clubs about players who have signed Academy Commitment Letters for that year.

9.6.1. Academy Commitment Letters During Fall Trial Window

During the Fall Trial Window, a player may sign an Academy Commitment Letter of one (1) of two (2) lengths with a Recruiting Club:

  • An Academy Commitment Letter to reserve the player's registration with the Recruiting Club's Academy for the immediately following January 1; or
  • An Academy Commitment Letter to reserve the player's registration with the Recruiting Club's Academy for the immediately following MLS NEXT Season.
9.6.2. Academy Commitment Letter for the Spring Trial Window

During the Spring Trial Window, a player may only sign an Academy Commitment Letter with a Recruiting Club to reserve his registration with the Recruiting Club's Academy for the immediately following MLS NEXT Season.

9.7. MLS Club Obligations for Academy Commitment Letters

9.7.1.

By signing a Recruitable Player to an MLS Academy Commitment Letter, an MLS Club's Academy must comply with the following:

  • Before or upon the next MLS NEXT Season or the immediate January 1, the MLS Club's Academy must register the player subject to MLS NEXT rules and regulations; and
  • Upon the next MLS NEXT Season or following the immediate January 1, the MLS Club must list the player on its Homegrown Player List for at least the next two (2) Homegrown Player List Submission Dates.
9.7.2.

After the League approves a fully-signed MLS Academy Commitment Letter, the League will include the player and his relevant information on a separate tab on the League's Homegrown Player List, which will be updated bi-monthly via MLS Connect. While the MLS Academy Commitment Letter remains effective, the player is not eligible to be recruited by other MLS Clubs.

Commentary: For Section 9.7.1a, the MLS Club's Academy must register the player within the immediately following MLS NEXT Season. Therefore, an Academy Commitment Letter to reserve the player's registration with the MLS Club's Academy for the immediately following January 1 is only applicable if the Academy Commitment Letter is signed during the fall MLS NEXT Season (August through December).

9.8. All Other Player Relocations Must Be for Non-Soccer Reasons

If a player who relocates from the Homegrown Territory of one MLS Club (the First MLS Club) to the Homegrown Territory of another MLS Club (the Second MLS Club) and if the Second MLS Club wants to register the player to its Academy, then the Second MLS Club must request written confirmation from the First MLS Club that the player relocated for non-soccer reasons and be prepared to demonstrate that the player relocated for non-soccer reasons. This obligation applies to the following categories of players:

  • A player who relocates as an Underage Player and is looking to register with an MLS Club's Academy for the first time
  • A player included on the First MLS Club's Homegrown Player List - Plus 5 who relocates from the First MLS Club's Homegrown Territory
  • A Registered Academy Player, regardless of being listed among The 35 of the First MLS Club's Homegrown Player List

If the First MLS Club does not provide written confirmation, then the Second MLS Club may add the player to its Academy and/or Homegrown Player List at its own risk.

Commentary: If the First MLS Club does not provide written confirmation that the player moved for non-soccer reasons, then the Second MLS Club risks tampering sanctions for the player's relocation. It is up to the Second MLS Club to conduct proper due diligence to minimize this risk, as the League will presume that the player relocated for soccer reasons if the First MLS Club files a claim. The Second MLS Club would have the burden to prove otherwise. MLS Clubs should note the Statute of Limitations for filing claims at Section 16.5.

G. Compliance

15. Breach of MLS Player Development Guidelines

An MLS Club's noncompliance with any of its obligations in these Guidelines may be considered a breach of these Guidelines and/or League Rules. Further, the following non-exhaustive conduct is considered a breach of these Guidelines, whether committed by the MLS Club or its MLS NEXT Pro team:

  • 15.1.1 Recruit, communicate, trial, register, or move or attempt to recruit, communicate, trial, register, or move a player on another MLS Club's Homegrown Player List;
  • 15.1.2 Recruit, communicate, trial, register, or move or attempt to recruit, communicate, trial, register, or move an Underage Player from another MLS Club's Homegrown Territory;
  • 15.1.3 Engage in conduct or attempt to engage in conduct that violates MLS Tampering Rules;
  • 15.1.4 Act in bad faith or dishonesty in managing or submitting its Homegrown Player List;
  • 15.1.5 Submit a late, inaccurate, incomplete, or otherwise noncompliant Homegrown Player List; and
  • 15.1.6 Engage in conduct that is intended to circumvent these Guidelines or obstruct the League's investigation into a potential breach of the Guidelines.

Commentary: Under MLS Tampering Rules, an MLS Club must not tamper with players over whom the MLS Clubs does not have priority rights without first obtaining written permission. Therefore, an MLS Club must not tamper with a player on the Homegrown Player List of another MLS Club or with Underage Players from another MLS Club's Homegrown Territory. Further, an MLS Club must not use its MLS NEXT Pro team to circumvent these Guidelines and/or League Rules. If a player or a player's family or representative initiates contact with an MLS Club and further contact between the player or player's family or representative and the MLS Club would violate the Guidelines and/or League Rules, then the MLS Club must immediately notify the League before engaging further. Failure to do so is considered a breach of these Guidelines and/or League Rules.

16. Claims

16.1. Filing a Claim

An MLS Club may file a dispute via MLS Connect with the League against another MLS Club regarding a potential breach of these Guidelines (a Claim). An MLS Club filing a Claim must commit to paying a bond of Five Thousand And No/100 Dollars (USD $5,000.00) following a League Decision that does not find that the Claim successfully shows that the Respondent Club breached these Guidelines.

16.2. Claim Requirements

Notwithstanding the mandatory fields and instructions detailed in the Claim form via MLS Connect, the Claim must include:

  • Name of MLS Club submitting the Claim (a Claimant Club),
  • Name of MLS Club against which the Claim is made (a Respondent Club)
  • Player's full name
  • Player's date of birth
  • All player home addresses and dates of residence relevant to the Claim
  • Home address of player's Parent(s) or Legal Guardian(s) and dates of residence (if different from above) relevant to the claim
  • Exact duration Player was registered in Academy
  • Reason(s) the Claimant Club is submitting the Claim
  • Factual information supporting the Claim
  • Evidence supporting the Claim, which must be attached, properly labeled, and comprise, where applicable:
    • (i) At least one piece of corroborating evidence confirming the player's home address;
    • (ii) Proof of the player's registration; and/or
    • (iii) Any relevant information or correspondence regarding the player/family or the Respondent Club
  • Confirmation that the bond payment of Five Thousand And No/00 Dollars (USD $5,000.00) will be posted if and when directed by the League.
Page 26

Commentary: The purpose of the bond is to ensure that an MLS Club does not file a frivolous claim. A Claim must be ripe, and the facts substantiated, otherwise the Claimant Club risks paying its full bond.

Commentary: Upon receiving the League's instructions, a Claimant Club must pay the bond to MLS by wire transfer in US dollars (USD) to the bank account details.

Commentary: Failure to meet the requirements above will result in an incomplete claim, which will not be reviewed by the League and, therefore, the Claimant Club will pay its full bond. Save for technical malfunctions, A Claimant Club will not have the opportunity to re-submit its claim; therefore, the Claimant Club should be overinclusive of any information or evidence it deems relevant to its Claim.

16.3. Response

16.3.1.

Following receipt of the Claim, the Respondent Club must respond within seven (7) days to the League via MLS Connect (a Response), which must state whether it accepts or denies each fact alleged.

16.3.2.

Where a material fact of the Claim is denied, the Respondent Club must provide a summary of any defense and evidence supporting its defense in accordance with Section 16.2.j above.

Commentary: For the avoidance of doubt, a Respondent Club's failure to respond or failure to respond according to the requirements above will be considered an admission of the breach.

16.4. League Decision

Where a Response is required as provided above, the League may:

  • 16.4.1. Further investigate the potential breach;
  • 16.4.2. Request additional information from the Club(s); and/or
  • 16.4.3. Where it determines that the Respondent Club has breached these Guidelines, issue a decision; or
  • 16.4.4. Where it determines that the Respondent Club did not breach these Guidelines, notify the relevant MLS Clubs participating in the dispute that the matter is concluded and take no further action. As a result, the Claimant Club must pay the full bond.

16.5. Statute of Limitations

A Claim must be filed within one (1) year from the later of: (A) the date the breach is asserted to have occurred or (B) the date that the MLS Club was notified or made reasonably aware that the asserted breach occurred (Statute of Limitations). Save for exceptional circumstances, an MLS Club is precluded from bringing a Claim outside of the Statute of Limitations.

17. Homegrown Player List Conflict Resolution

17.1. Players Who Appear on More Than One List – League Redress

If a player is included on more than one MLS Club's Homegrown Player List, the League will determine, on a case-by-case basis, whether to grant or revoke Homegrown Exclusivity based on the following (non-exhaustive) factors:

  • Whether the player is registered to an MLS Club;
  • Whether the player was formerly registered to an MLS Club;
  • The player's parent's home address (and recent history of home addresses);
  • Whether an MLS Club engaged in conduct that is considered a potential breach of these Guidelines.

17.2. Players Who Appear on More Than One List – MLS Club Redress

Further to Section 17.1, if an MLS Club alleges that another MLS Club engaged in conduct that is considered a potential breach of the Guidelines, the alleging MLS Club may:

  • Where applicable, include the player on its Homegrown List;
  • Where applicable, negotiate a settlement with the other MLS Club; or
  • File a Claim under the requirements set out in Section 16.

18. Academy Commitment Letters

The following provisions will apply if a player who signed an Academy Commitment Letter with an MLS Club's Academy ultimately does not register:

  • 18.1.1. If the MLS Club unilaterally refuses or fails to register the player and/or to include the player on its Homegrown Player List, the MLS Club will lose two (2) Homegrown Player List Slots for the next two (2) Homegrown Player List Submission Dates.
  • 18.1.2. The Academy Commitment Letter is not binding on the player. However, if the player unilaterally refuses or fails to register with the MLS Club's Academy and instead registers with a different MLS Club's Academy, then the MLS Club with which he signed an Academy Commitment Letter may protect its rights by filing a claim.
  • 18.1.3. If the player and the MLS Club's Academy mutually agree to void the signed Academy Commitment Letter, then the player and the MLS Club's Academy must first notify the MLS Player Relations Department at Homegrown.PlayerRelations@mlssoccer.com in writing no less than one week prior to the opening of the next Homegrown Player List Submission Date. Following such notice, the League will void the signed Academy Commitment Letter and it will have no further effect.

19. Investigations and Enforcement

19.1. League Discretion

The League may investigate any allegation or suspicion of a breach of these Guidelines, whether reported by an MLS Club or otherwise.

Page 28

19.2. MLS Club Document Production

Where the League decides to investigate, the League may require an MLS Club to produce documents or correspondence within the MLS Club's power, possession, custody and/or control.

19.3. MLS Commissioner's Authority

Without prejudice to the enforcement mechanisms detailed in these Guidelines, the Roster and Budget Guidelines or any League Rules, if following its investigation of an MLS Club the League determines that the MLS Club breached these Guidelines, the matter may subsequently be referred to the MLS Commissioner or his designee, who may impose penalties or sanctions under League Rules.

20. Decisions and Sanctions

At any time, the League may issue a decision against an MLS Club in breach of these Guidelines (a Decision). A Decision by the League is final. If a Decision includes a sanction, the sanction will be assessed based on the severity of the breach and the number of breaches committed by the MLS Club in a designated period, and may result in the following non-exhaustive list of sanctions:

  • A reprimand or warning regarding future conduct;
  • Public disclosure to all MLS Clubs;
  • Removal of Homegrown Player slots – The 35 and/or Plus 5;
  • An allocation penalty payable to another MLS Club; and/or
  • A financial penalty payable to the League, which must be paid within thirty (30) days of the date of the Decision Letter sent to the MLS Club.

21. Confidentiality and Public Discourse

21.1. Confidentiality Requirement

All investigations, Claims, Responses, and Decisions will be conducted on a private and confidential basis between the League and the relevant MLS Club(s). However, the League may notify other MLS Clubs of any Decision or findings.

21.2. No Public Comment

An MLS Club must not publicly comment on any compliance matter detailed in these Guidelines.

Training Compensation – Updated MLS Guidance

FROM: MLS Player Relations

RE: Training Compensation – Updated MLS Guidance

The League Office has recently reviewed its existing guidance to MLS Clubs entitled to Training Compensation under the FIFA Regulations on the Status and Transfer of Players (the "FIFA RSTP") for players trained in their Academies.

To ensure our player development efforts reflect the current FIFA Training Compensation and Solidarity landscape and the change in process due to the implementation of the FIFA Clearing House, League Office guidance to MLS Clubs going forward is the following:

  • Where an MLS Club is entitled to Training Compensation under the FIFA RSTP for a player trained in its Academy (and/or with its MLS NEXT Pro team) who did not sign an MLS contract, the MLS Club may receive such compensation regardless of whether it offered the player an MLS contract.
  • If an MLS player seeks to join a non-MLS club as an "out-of-contract" player following termination of his MLS contract, the MLS Club must waive Training Compensation if so requested by the player's new club.
  • MLS Academies must continue to require all players and their parent(s)/guardian(s) to sign the Acknowledgment of Player Obligations Memo each year as a condition of their acceptance into the Academy.

Below, we provide further detail on the points above. Please note that this guidance only applies to claims for training compensation under the FIFA RSTP and does not apply to MLS Right of First Refusal rules or to claims for solidarity payments under the FIFA RSTP.

Page 36

Not Required to Offer an MLS Contract to Receive Training Compensation

MLS is rescinding its prior guidance requiring MLS/an MLS Club to offer an MLS contract to a player to receive Training Compensation for his development. Subject to the exception detailed below, this generally means an MLS Club may claim training compensation for its former Academy (and/or MLS NEXT Pro) player who was offered: (i) an MLS contract, (ii) an MLS NEXT Pro contract, or (iii) no contract at all.

Must Waive Training Compensation for MLS Players if Requested

Under Section 15.4 of the MLS Collective Bargaining Agreement, a former MLS player must be free to sign a contract with any professional non-MLS club without MLS or an MLS Club requiring payment of Training Compensation. If requested by the player's new club, MLS and MLS Clubs must agree to waive Training Compensation and provide the necessary documentation for submission to the FIFA Clearing House.

This waiver requirement does not apply to players who only signed an MLS NEXT Pro contract. Clubs should act reasonably given the circumstances, reviewing the amount of Training Compensation at issue for a particular player and consider waiving it if requested.

Academy Players Must Sign Acknowledgment of Player Obligations Memo Each Year

MLS Club Academies must continue to require all players and their parent(s)/guardian(s) to sign the Acknowledgment of Player Obligations Memo each year as a condition of their acceptance into the Academy. As a reminder, the Acknowledgment of Player Obligations Memo details, among other things, the following:

  • The player acknowledges that if he signs a professional contract with a non-MLS club outside of the U.S. or Canada, then the MLS Academy will be compensated for his development through the FIFA Clearing House, calculated according to the FIFA RSTP.
  • The player also acknowledges that future international transfers of the player may result in the MLS Academy receiving Solidarity payments through the FIFA Clearing House, calculated according to the FIFA RSTP.
  • The player must notify his MLS Academy if he receives a communication from a non-MLS club.
  • If the player receives an offer from a non-MLS club, the player must notify his MLS Academy, and MLS will have up to fourteen (14) calendar days to offer him an employment agreement.
